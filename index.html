Keith Christopher Rowley: the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago’s 7th Prime Minister

November 19, 2019November 19, 2019| Dana McKeonDana McKeon| 0 Comment

On October 24, 1949, Dr. Keith Christopher Rowley was born in Mason Hall, Tobago. He was raised by his grandparents who were one of Tobago’s influential farmers. He studied from Bishop’s High School for his secondary education, and his outstanding student achievement won him the coveted BHS Sylvan Bowles Scholarship. He then went on to study at West Indies University, Mona, Jamaica, and graduated with a B.Sc. Geology where he was a first-class honor, Geography (also a first-class honor); MSc, UWI, St. Augustine, Volcanic Stratigraphy.

He has worked with dozens of positions as a research scientist, namely Research Fellow and Head of the Seismic Research Unit at St. Augustine University in the West Indies. He was also the general manager of the National Quarry Company Limited, which is owned by the State.

He was thinking of pursuing the political arena and decided to run to challenge Tobago West as his first candidate in 1981. To date, Keith Rowley has the advantage of having won the general election in both Tobago and Trinidad as the sole candidate of the People’s National Movement (PNM).

He has become a member of the House of Representatives of the Parliament of Trinidad and Tobago for Diego Martin West, where he has served the electorate since 1991, having been re-elected in 1995, 2000, 2001, 2002, 2007 and 2010.

Shortly after the loss of the National People’s Movement in the 2010 Trinidad and Tobago General Election, Dr. Rowley was elected Opposition Leader of the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago and then National People’s Movement Political Leader in 2010. He helped lead the National People’s Movement to a general election victory on 7 September 2015, becoming the seventh Prime Minister of the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago.
